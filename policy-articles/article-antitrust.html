The Antitrust Case Against Big Tech: A Status Report

From the Google search decision to Meta's FTC showdown, the antitrust case against Big Tech is moving — slowly. Here's where each major action stands and what remedies might actually change the industry.

The Department of Justice and Federal Trade Commission have brought five major antitrust cases against the four largest tech companies. The cases target different conduct in different markets, but the underlying theory is the same: the platform monopolies of the 2010s used exclusionary practices to lock in their dominance, and a generation of innovation has been lost as a result. The courts are starting to agree. The question now is remedy — and that's where the fight gets ugly.

Google Search: Liability Found, Remedy Pending

In August 2024, Judge Amit Mehta found Google liable for monopolizing the search market through exclusionary distribution agreements with Apple, Samsung, and Mozilla. The remedy trial concluded in late 2025. The DOJ is asking for structural relief: a forced divestiture of Chrome and potentially Android. Google is arguing for behavioral remedies only. A decision is expected in Q3 2026. Whatever the outcome, this case will set the template for every tech antitrust action that follows.

Google Ad Tech: The Bigger Threat

The more consequential case may be the parallel ad-tech action, also before Judge Mehta. In April 2025, the court found Google liable for monopolizing two distinct markets: publisher ad servers and ad exchanges. The DOJ has now asked for a forced divestiture of Google's ad exchange (AdX) and possibly DoubleClick for Publishers. If granted, this would break the integrated ad stack that powers most of the open web. Publishers are cautiously optimistic; advertisers are alarmed.

Apple: The App Store Fight

Apple faces a different posture: a private antitrust case (Epic v. Apple) resulted in a 2024 injunction prohibiting Apple's anti-steering provisions. The injunction has been partially appealed and partially strengthened. The bigger threat is the DOJ's 2025 case alleging Apple monopolized the smartphone market — a much broader theory that, if successful, would open the door to forced App Store sideloading on a permanent basis. Trial is set for early 2027.

Meta: The FTC's Long Game

The FTC's case against Meta, originally filed in 2020, has been to procedural trip. The agency lost its first attempt at trial, won on appeal, and is now preparing for a retrial focused on the Instagram and WhatsApp acquisitions. The remedies the FTC will seek — a forced divestiture of Instagram, possibly WhatsApp — are dramatic. Meta's defense is that the acquisitions were lawful at the time and that the products have only improved under its ownership. The retrial is scheduled for fall 2026.

"The question is no longer whether Big Tech has monopoly power — courts have agreed. The question is whether American antitrust law is willing to do what it took to break Standard Oil and AT&T: actually break them up."

Amazon: The Quiet Case

The FTC's case against Amazon, filed in 2023, has been the slowest to move. The agency alleges that Amazon's marketplace practices (self-preferencing, fee structures that punish off-platform selling, the Buy Box algorithm) have foreclosed competition. Trial is scheduled for 2027. The remedy, if the FTC wins, could include structural separation of Amazon's retail and marketplace businesses — a far more radical outcome than the company has ever faced.

What Changes If The Government Wins

If the courts grant the structural remedies the agencies are asking for, the result will be a fundamental restructuring of the consumer internet. A Chrome without Google search would mean Bing or DuckDuckGo as default for hundreds of millions of users. A divested AdX would mean publishers finally get fair market access. A separated Instagram or WhatsApp would mean new independent competitors in social and messaging. The next decade of consumer tech will be defined by which remedies are granted and how they're implemented. The metallic age of platforms is about to be remelted.
